This guide covers everything you need to manage your team during a CERT activation — from opening the dashboard and tracking member check-ins, to creating sub-teams, assigning tasks, monitoring the live map, and responding to field reports.

Open a browser on your laptop or tablet and go to your team's dashboard URL:

Two separate PINs: Your dashboard PIN is unique to your team and grants full administrative access. The member PIN (001789) allows field members to check in and submit reports only. Never share your dashboard PIN with field members.

Enter your PIN and click Enter Dashboard. The PIN is case-sensitive.

Use a tablet or laptop in landscape mode for the best experience. The dashboard works on phones but the layout is optimized for larger screens.
Dashboard data is stored in memory on the server. If the server restarts, all check-in data (members, tasks, sub-teams) is cleared. The audit log (ICS-214) is permanently saved and survives restarts.

2. Activating and Deactivating CERT

Before your team is visible to the County EOC, you must explicitly activate your team. This tells the county that your team is operational and should appear on the county dashboard.

1
Click ACTIVATE CERT at the top of the dashboard A gray banner reads "⚪ CERT INACTIVE — Not visible to County EOC" when your team is not yet activated. Click the green ACTIVATE CERT button.
2
Confirm the activation prompt A confirmation dialog appears. Click OK to activate. The banner turns green: 🟢 CERT ACTIVATED — Visible to County EOC.
3
At incident end: click DEACTIVATE CERT When your team stands down, click DEACTIVATE CERT and confirm. Your team will be removed from the county EOC view. Member data, reports, and tasks are preserved.
Activation is persistent. If you close the dashboard browser and come back, your team's activation status is maintained — you do not need to re-activate unless the server container was restarted.
No activation = not visible to county. If you forget to activate, the county coordinator will not see your team on the county dashboard even if members are checked in.

The dashboard updates in real time via WebSocket — no refresh needed.

5. Managing Member Check-Ins

Members check in via the web portal at [team].certcommand.org/member or via the iPhone app. They appear in your Members list within seconds.

✏️
Editing a member's name Click the member's name to edit it inline — useful if someone checked in with a typo or used a nickname.
🗑
Checking out a member remotely Use the remove/check-out button on a member card to check them out from the dashboard. The member is immediately notified — their phone shows an alert saying "Your Team Leader has checked you out" and their session ends automatically. This works for both web and iPhone app users.
Remote check-out notification is delivered within 10 seconds. The member does not need to do anything — their screen returns to the check-in page automatically.

6. Creating Sub-Teams

1
Click "Create Sub-Team" Choose a color for the sub-team (Red, Blue, Green, Orange, Purple, Teal, Yellow, Pink). The color becomes the team's visual identifier across the dashboard and members' phones.
2
Assign members to the sub-team Select which checked-in members belong to this sub-team. A member can only be in one sub-team at a time.
3
Member badge updates automatically Within 10 seconds, each assigned member's phone shows a colored badge: "Red Team", "Blue Team", etc. No action needed from members.
4
Reassign or move members You can reassign a member to a different sub-team at any time from their member card. Their badge updates on their phone automatically.

7. Creating and Assigning Tasks

1
Click "Create Task" Enter the task title, description, and priority level (Low / Medium / High / Critical).
2
Assign to a member or sub-team Select who the task goes to. Members see their task on their phone within 10 seconds.
3
Track task status Tasks progress through: Open → Assigned → Completed. Members can add comments to tasks from their phone.
4
Edit or cancel a task Click on any task to edit, reassign, or cancel it.

8. Viewing and Replying to Reports

Members submit ICS-213 General Message reports from their phones or the web portal. These appear in the Reports section.

1
Click on any report to open it The detail shows all ICS-213 fields: Subject, From, Incident Type, Severity, Location, and Description.
2
Send a reply Type your response in the Reply field and click Send Reply. The member sees it on their phone within 10 seconds.
3
Acknowledge or escalate Use Acknowledge to mark as reviewed. Use Escalate to County to forward to the county coordination dashboard.
4
Export the report as ICS-213 Every report has a 📋 ICS-213 button in the action row at the bottom of the detail view. Clicking it opens a new tab with a fully formatted, printable FEMA ICS-213 General Message form, pre-filled with the report data, reply, and county acknowledgment status.
Report BadgeMeaning
CriticalRequires immediate attention
HighUrgent — respond soon
MediumMonitor and respond when able
LowFYI / informational
ICS-213 forms can be exported from both the team leader's dashboard and the county EOC dashboard. Use them for after-action documentation, insurance records, or incident filing.

9. Escalating to County

For incidents requiring county-level coordination, escalate from the report detail view.

1
Open the report
2
Click "Escalate to County" The report is forwarded to the County EOC dashboard at county.certcommand.org instantly. The report is flagged as escalated on your dashboard.
County Dashboard URL: https://county.certcommand.org
The county coordinator's PIN is provided separately — do not include it in member-facing materials.

10. Incident Log & ICS-214 Export

Every significant event during an activation is automatically logged: check-ins, check-outs, status changes, reports, task assignments, replies, and sub-team creation.

📄
View the Incident Log The Incident Log panel on the dashboard shows a timestamped event history for the current activation.
🖨
Export ICS-214 Click "Export ICS-214" to generate a FEMA/NIMS-compliant Activity Log in printable format. Includes the ICS-211 Check-In/Check-Out roster.
The incident log is stored permanently in the database and survives server restarts. You can export the ICS-214 at any time, even after the activation ends.

11. Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Member checked in but not showingHard refresh the dashboard (Shift+Refresh). If the server restarted, the member needs to re-check in.
Dashboard shows no data after restartIn-memory data is cleared on restart. Have all members re-check in. The ICS-214 log is preserved.
WebSocket disconnected (no live updates)Refresh the page and re-enter your PIN — this reconnects the WebSocket.
Member not appearing on mapMember may be using the web browser (no GPS). Ask them to use the iPhone app and enable location sharing.
Report not showing county escalationCheck that county.certcommand.org loads. If it doesn't, the county server may be down.
Locked out after wrong PINWait 1 hour for automatic unlock, or contact the system administrator.
