CERT Command

County EOC Coordinator Guide

County Edition
certcommand.org  ·  v1.3  ·  2026

This guide covers the County EOC (Emergency Operations Center) dashboard — the unified view of all CERT field teams. From here you can monitor every team's status, receive escalated incident reports, send alerts and messages to individual teams or all teams at once, and reply to field reports that have been forwarded to county level.

1. Accessing the County Dashboard

Open a browser on your laptop or tablet and navigate to:

https://county.certcommand.org

Enter the county coordinator PIN when prompted. The PIN is case-sensitive.

The county PIN provides access to all five team feeds simultaneously. Only share it with authorized county-level coordinators — never with field members or team leaders.
Use a large monitor or laptop in landscape mode. The county dashboard shows all five teams side by side and benefits from a wide display.

2. Dashboard Overview

The county dashboard is divided into two areas:

AreaWhat It Shows
Team Cards (left/main panel)One card per activated team showing member count, open task count, active reports by severity, and last-update time. Click any card to open the team's full detail view.
Detail Panel (right/overlay)After clicking a team card: their full member roster, task list, and all reports. Also includes the "Send Message to Team" form.

The dashboard updates automatically — no refresh needed.

Teams must be explicitly activated to appear here. A team only shows on the county dashboard after the team leader clicks ACTIVATE CERT on their own dashboard. Teams that haven't been activated — or that have deactivated at incident end — are not shown, even if they have members checked in.

3. Reading the Team Cards

Each team card is color-coded on its left border to indicate the highest-severity open report from that team:

Red border
Life-safety report — immediate action needed
Orange border
High-severity report open
Yellow border
Medium-severity report open
Green border
Low-severity report or no reports
Gray / dimmed card
Team server unreachable — no recent data

Each card also shows:

  • Member count — how many members are currently checked in
  • Open task count — tasks not yet completed or cancelled
  • Report counts — number of open reports by severity level
  • Pending messages — messages you've sent to this team that haven't been confirmed yet
  • Last updated — timestamp of the most recent data received from this team

4. Viewing a Team's Detail

Click on any team card to open its full detail panel.

⚠
Priority Reports Reports that need acknowledgment appear at the top in red/orange. These are the reports that have been escalated by the team leader or that are critical/high severity.
📋
Other Reports All other open reports for this team, sorted by severity.
✅
Open Tasks Tasks currently assigned within this team.
👥
Members All checked-in members with their name, role, and current status.

5. Receiving Escalated Reports

Team leaders can escalate any incident report up to the county level. When a report is escalated:

  • The report appears in the team's detail panel under Priority Reports
  • The team card's border updates to reflect the escalated report's severity
  • The originating team's dashboard shows the report as "Escalated"
Escalated reports remain visible on both the team dashboard and the county dashboard until acknowledged or resolved. They are not removed from the team's view when the county receives them.

6. Sending Messages to Teams

You can send messages to a single team or to all teams at once. Messages are delivered within 30 seconds and appear as a banner notification on the team leader's dashboard.

Send a message to one team

1
Click on the team's card Open the team's detail panel.
2
Scroll to "Send Message to Team" This section is at the bottom of the detail panel.
3
Choose the message type
TypeWhen to Use
⚠️ AlertUrgent operational message requiring immediate team attention. Displayed prominently on the team dashboard.
ℹ️ InfoGeneral information, situational update, or non-urgent coordination message.
✅ Ack / ReplyAcknowledgment of a report or response to something the team has submitted.
4
Type your message and click Send The message is queued for delivery. The team leader's dashboard will show it within 30 seconds, when the team's server next polls the county for messages.

Send a message to all teams at once

Use the broadcast message section at the top of the county dashboard (outside any team detail panel) to send the same message to all five teams simultaneously. This is useful for county-wide alerts, activation updates, or stand-down notices.

Delivery is not instantaneous — each team's server polls county every 30 seconds. For truly time-critical information, follow up by radio or phone if immediate acknowledgment is needed.

7. Replying to Escalated Reports

When a team leader escalates a report to county, you can send a reply that goes back to that team leader's dashboard.

1
Open the team that escalated the report Click the team's card.
2
Find the escalated report in Priority Reports
3
Use "Send Message to Team" with type "Ack / Reply" Reference the report subject so the team leader understands what you're responding to.
County replies appear as a message on the team leader's dashboard. The original field member who submitted the report also sees the team leader's reply on their own phone (if the team leader chose to forward it).

8. Exporting ICS-213 General Message Forms

Any incident report visible in a team's detail panel can be exported as a FEMA ICS-213 General Message form — the standard written communication record used in ICS documentation. This is useful for after-action review, legal/insurance documentation, or printing a paper record of county–team communications.

How to export an ICS-213

1
Open a team's detail panel Click any team card to open their full report view.
2
Find the report and click 📋 ICS-213 Each report row — both in Priority Reports and Other Reports — has a small ICS-213 button on the right side.
3
A new tab opens with the completed form The ICS-213 is pre-filled with the report's data. Click 🖨️ Print / Save as PDF at the bottom of the page to print or save.

The form is populated as follows:

ICS-213 BoxFilled From
1 — Incident NameTeam name + "CERT Incident"
2 — ToCounty EOC (Emergency Operations Center)
3 — FromReporter name and position (from the team leader's report)
4 — SubjectReport subject if set, otherwise report type and severity
7 — MessageReport type, severity, location, and full notes
8 — Approved BySignature line (blank for handwritten signature on printed copy)
9 — ReplyTeam leader's written reply if one was submitted; otherwise notes if county acknowledged
10/11 — Replied By / DateReply author name and date/time if available
If your browser blocks the pop-up, allow pop-ups for county.certcommand.org and click the ICS-213 button again.
The team leader can also export ICS-213 forms from their dashboard for any report they have open. Both sides produce the same form from the same data.

9. Monitoring Team Status

The county dashboard is designed for continuous passive monitoring. Keep it open throughout the activation and watch for:

SignalWhat It Means
Team card border turns redA life-safety report has been opened. Click the card immediately.
Team card is dimmed/grayThe team's server isn't responding. Contact the team leader by radio.
Member count drops to 0All members have checked out — team may have stood down or the server restarted.
Pending message badge on cardA message you sent hasn't been confirmed by that team's server yet — may still be in transit.
A dimmed (gray) team card does not necessarily mean an emergency — the team server may have restarted or lost connectivity. Always confirm via radio before drawing conclusions from a gray card.

10. Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Team card is gray / staleThe county server can't reach that team's server. Try refreshing the county dashboard. If still gray, contact the team leader by radio — their server may need to be restarted.
Message shows as pending for more than 2 minutesThe team server polls every 30 seconds. If pending longer, the team server may be down. Confirm by radio.
Escalated report not appearingHard-refresh the county dashboard (Shift+Refresh). Escalated reports may take up to 30 seconds to appear after the team leader sends them.
Dashboard not updatingRefresh the page and re-enter the county PIN. The WebSocket may have disconnected.
Locked out after wrong PINWait 1 hour for automatic unlock, or contact the system administrator.
